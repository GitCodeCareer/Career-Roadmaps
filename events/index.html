Frequently Asked Questions

If you don't see an answer; reach out @benjaminspak

What will be the main focus of this hackathon?
Build anything that will empower other makers, hackers, developers or designers.
What could we do to support the hackathon?

Social media marketing of the event.


We also recommend a Twitch stream so you can feature teams projects, jump in their chat & help with code questions.

What's the time-frame for the event?
Strict UTC timeline December 28th @ 10:00 PM UTC to December 31st @ 05:00 AM UTC.
Can I join the event late?

Yes & no. To be considered for prizes & awards you must register before DEC 28TH @ 10pm UTC.


However, even if you're not part of the official hackathon, you are welcome to join in the community events & build along side participants.

What's the purpose of this event? Why did you organize it?

We decided to create a hackathon listing on DevPost.com.

Turns out online hackathons cost $1500 to host on their platform.


So we decided; "let's make our own platform".

How many people do you expect to participate?
Conservatively; we expect about 25 people to participate.
What level of exposure do you expect for the event?

For an idea of exposure; conservatively, I typically receive a minimum of 1,500 views for each Tweet from my personal account @benjaminspak.


I will be Tweeting sponsors information leading up to and during the event. We have also partnered with a couple of rising startups maker.rocks & makerlog.


producthunt.com/posts/makerlog

producthunt.com/posts/maker-rocks


Additionally, I partnered with a hungry & rapidly growing web media company MakersUp for Twitter coverage & Twitch coverage of the event.


twitter.com/makersup


We landed Product Hunt as a sponsor, as well. Most participants will be sharing they startups & projects on the Product Hunt to get more exposure for their launch.


producthunt.com


We are requiring all participants to stream their progress via Twitch. A conservative estimate is about 25 views per participant.


We are also running a main Twitch stream that we will use to interview panelists, host AMA's & occasionally drop in on participants streams so they can tell us about their project's progress.


Keep in mind, Code Career & all streamers will archive the video footage on their Twitch channel that will bring in additional views after the event.


I will also, personally be reaching out to several tech publications online such as freeCodeCamp & Hacker Noon to get more pre-event exposure.


And, typically more news sources will write about the event afterward.


Can my company participate?
Sure! As long as your team is under 3 people.
